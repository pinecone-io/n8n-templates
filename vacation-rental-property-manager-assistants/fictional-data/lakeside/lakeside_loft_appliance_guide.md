## ☕ Coffee Maker — Espresso Machine + Brewer Combo

**Location:** Kitchen counter, left of the sink
**Model:** ViaCaffe Dual Pro (VC-DP2)

The ViaCaffe is a **dual-function machine**: it makes both espresso drinks (left side) and a full 10-cup drip pot (right side). Ground coffee and espresso beans are in the sealed tins on the counter — labeled clearly.

---

### SIDE A: Espresso

#### Making a Shot of Espresso

1. Press **POWER** — the machine takes 90 seconds to heat up. The **READY** light turns solid green when ready (it blinks while heating).
2. Fill the **portafilter** (the handle with the small basket): scoop in **1 rounded tablespoon** of espresso grounds for a single shot; **2 tablespoons** for a double.
3. Tamp the grounds firmly with the tamper (black disc, in the drawer) — press straight down with moderate force until the surface is flat and level.
4. Lock the portafilter into the group head (twist clockwise until it stops firmly).
5. Place your espresso cup under the spout.
6. Press **SINGLE** or **DOUBLE** shot button. Extraction takes approximately 25–30 seconds.
7. A well-pulled shot is deep amber/red-brown with a tan crema on top. If it looks pale and watery, the grind is too coarse or the tamp was too light.

#### Steaming Milk (for Lattes & Cappuccinos)

1. Fill the small metal pitcher (in the cabinet) with cold milk — no more than halfway.
2. Purge the steam wand first: turn the steam knob briefly until a short burst of steam exits (1–2 seconds), then stop.
3. Submerge the steam wand tip just below the milk surface. Turn the steam knob fully open.
4. Keep the wand near the surface for 3–4 seconds to create foam (you'll hear a hissing paper-tearing sound). Then lower the wand deeper to heat the milk, swirling in a slow circle.
5. Target temperature: **140–150°F** (feels very hot but not burning to the touch).
6. Turn off steam, remove pitcher, and wipe the wand with the damp cloth immediately.
7. Tap the pitcher on the counter and swirl to integrate foam before pouring.

#### Quick Drinks Guide

| Drink | Espresso | Milk | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Espresso | 1–2 shots | None | Straight into small cup |
| Americano | 1–2 shots | Hot water (equal to shots) | Fill cup with hot water first |
| Latte | 2 shots | 6 oz steamed milk, light foam | Pour milk over shots |
| Cappuccino | 2 shots | 3 oz steamed milk, heavy foam | Equal parts espresso/milk/foam |
| Flat White | 2 shots | 4 oz microfoam | Silky, no dry foam |
| Macchiato | 1 shot | 1 oz foamed milk on top | Small; very strong |

---

### SIDE B: Drip Coffee

1. Open the filter basket on the right side of the machine (flip lid up).
2. Insert a **#4 cone filter** (in the cabinet above) and add **1 tablespoon of ground coffee per 6 oz of water**.
3. Fill the carafe with cold water and pour into the water reservoir (marked with cup levels on the side).
4. Close the filter basket. Place carafe on the warming plate.
5. Press **BREW** (right side button, blue). Brewing takes 6–8 minutes for a full pot.
6. The **Keep Warm** function maintains temperature for 2 hours, then shuts off.

#### Cleaning
- Wipe the steam wand thoroughly after every use — milk burns on quickly.
- Rinse the portafilter and filter basket with warm water daily.
- Run a **rinse cycle** (empty portafilter, press single shot) to flush the group head.
- Descale monthly: use the ViaCaffe descale packets under the sink. Instructions on the packet.

## 🍟 Air Fryer — Convection Oven with Air Fry Mode

**Location:** Under-counter pull-out shelf, right side of kitchen
**Model:** OmniChef Pro Convection Oven (OC-25XL)

This is a **countertop convection oven** with a dedicated Air Fry mode — it functions as a full oven, toaster, broiler, and air fryer in one unit. Pull the shelf out before use (there's a handle on the front of the shelf).

### Control Panel Overview

The control panel has a dial and 6 mode buttons:

| Button | Mode |
|---|---|
| AIR FRY | High-speed circulating heat (most "fryer" results) |
| BAKE | Standard convection bake |
| BROIL | Top heating element only |
| TOAST | Top + bottom elements, timer-based |
| ROAST | Convection roast with probe option |
| WARM | Keeps food warm at 170°F |

**Dial:** Outer ring = Temperature (170–450°F). Inner dial = Time (up to 60 min).

---

### Using Air Fry Mode

1. Pull out the shelf and slide out the **air fry basket** (the mesh tray with handles).
2. Place food in the basket in a **single layer** — do not stack or overcrowd.
3. Slide basket back into position 2 (middle rack, labeled on the side).
4. Press **AIR FRY**.
5. Turn temp dial to desired temperature.
6. Turn time dial to desired cook time.
7. Press **START** — the fan is loud in this mode. That's normal.
8. Shake or flip food halfway through cooking for even crispness.
9. Unit beeps at the end of cycle and fan stops automatically.

### Quick Reference (Air Fry Mode)

| Food | Temp | Time | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Frozen fries | 380°F | 14–16 min | Shake at 7 min |
| Shrimp | 400°F | 8–10 min | Pat dry; light oil |
| Chicken breast | 375°F | 18–22 min | Check to 165°F internal |
| Salmon | 390°F | 10–12 min | Skin side down |
| Veggies (dense) | 380°F | 12–15 min | Broccoli, brussels, etc. |
| Veggies (soft) | 360°F | 8–10 min | Zucchini, asparagus, etc. |
| Reheated leftovers | 325°F | 5–7 min | Far better than microwave |
| Frozen egg rolls | 370°F | 10–12 min | Flip once |
| Grilled cheese | 350°F | 6–8 min | Use BAKE mode instead |

### Cleaning
- Allow to cool fully before cleaning (30+ minutes).
- The basket, mesh tray, and drip pan are **dishwasher safe**.
- Wipe interior with a damp cloth. Do not immerse the unit.
- Remove crumb tray from the bottom and empty after each use.

## 🫧 Washer & Dryer — SparkleTwin Combo Washer/Dryer

**Location:** Utility nook off the hallway (behind the louvered doors)
**Model:** SparkleTwin All-in-One Washer/Dryer (ST-27AIO)

> ⚠️ **Important:** This is an all-in-one combo unit — it washes **and** dries in the same drum. No transferring clothes. Read instructions before use.
> 
> ⚠️ **HE Detergent ONLY.** HE pods are on the shelf above the unit. Do not use regular detergent — it causes excessive suds and error codes.

---

### How It Works

This machine washes, then automatically transitions to drying without you needing to do anything. A full wash + dry cycle takes **2.5 to 3.5 hours** depending on the load size and settings. Plan accordingly!

---

### Basic Operation

1. Open the **front porthole door** (pull handle firmly).
2. Load clothes loosely. **Max load: 10 lbs.** Due to the combo nature, smaller loads dry more effectively — 6–8 lbs is ideal.
3. Add **1 HE pod** to the drum (just toss it in, before clothes) OR add HE liquid to the small dispenser drawer above the door (the II compartment = main wash).
4. Close the door until it clicks.
5. Turn the **PROGRAM** dial:
   - **Wash+Dry (Auto)** – Full wash and dry cycle (recommended for most loads)
   - **Wash Only** – Washes but does not dry (if you prefer to air dry)
   - **Dry Only** – Dries without washing
   - **Quick Wash** – 30-minute light wash (items still need drying after)
   - **Delicates** – Gentle wash + low heat dry
   - **Rinse+Spin** – Rinse and spin only, no heat dry
6. Press **START/PAUSE**.
7. The door locks. Do not force it open during a cycle.
8. When the **END** light illuminates and the beeping starts, the cycle is complete. The door unlocks automatically.

### Drying Tips for Combo Units

Combo washer/dryers are slower at drying than stand-alone dryers. A few tips to get the best results:

- **Smaller loads dry better.** If you have a large amount, consider running two smaller cycles.
- After the cycle ends, items may feel slightly damp. Run **Dry Only** for 30–45 additional minutes to finish.
- The lint filter in combo units is a small mesh panel inside the door rim. **Clean before every use** — pull it out, peel off lint, reinsert.
- Cotton and heavy items (towels, jeans) take longer. Use the **Cotton High** heat setting for these.
- Delicates and synthetics dry faster and should use **Low Heat**.

### Control Panel Reference

#### Heat Level (press HEAT button to cycle)
- **High** – Cottons, towels, heavy items
- **Medium** – Blends, everyday clothes
- **Low** – Delicates, synthetics, workout clothes
- **No Heat/Air** – Very delicate; line-dry items

#### Spin Speed (press SPIN button to cycle)
- **High** – Less drying time needed; good for cottons
- **Medium** – Everyday default
- **Low** – Delicates; reduces wrinkles

### Troubleshooting

| Code/Issue | Meaning | Solution |
|---|---|---|
| **E2** | Door not sealed | Push door firmly until click |
| **E4** | Water inlet issue | Check that the water valve behind unit is fully open |
| **E7** | Excessive suds | Use HE detergent only; run Rinse+Spin cycle |
| **dH** | Over-heat in drying | Open door, let cool 15 min, restart Dry Only on Low |
| Clothes damp after cycle | Normal for combo unit | Run Dry Only for 30–45 extra minutes |
| Long cycle times | Normal | Combo units are slower than separate machines; plan ~3 hrs |
| Machine vibrating heavily | Unbalanced load | Pause, redistribute clothes evenly, restart |
| Unit won't start | — | Check door is latched; check power plug |
