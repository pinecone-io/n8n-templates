## Welcome to Lakeside Loft!

Thank you for choosing Lakeside Loft — I'm so excited for you to experience one of Lakeford's most unique stays. This converted boathouse loft sits directly on Lakeford Bay with panoramic water views, an private dock, and the most spectacular sunsets you'll ever see from a living room couch.

The building was originally a working boathouse from the 1940s. I've renovated the upper level into a modern open-plan loft while preserving the original timber frame and nautical character. I think you'll find it's a little bit magical.

Please read through the guide — there are a few quirks unique to a converted waterfront building that are helpful to know!

## House Rules

1. **No smoking** anywhere inside or on the private deck. The dock and public areas are smoke-free per city ordinance.
2. **No parties or events.** Maximum occupancy is 4 guests. The building has other permanent residents — please be respectful neighbors.
3. **No pets.** The building HOA does not allow pets for short-term rental guests. No exceptions.
4. **Quiet hours:** 10:00 PM – 8:00 AM. Sound travels easily over water and in the building.
5. **Dock safety:** The private dock is for use by registered guests only. No running on the dock. Children must wear life jackets when on the dock (PFDs are in the bench storage at the dock entrance). Do not dive from the dock.
6. **No outside guests overnight.** Your reservation is for named guests only.
7. **Kitchen:** Please do not move the kitchen island — it is connected to plumbing below.
8. **Windows facing the water:** These open fully. Please do not lean out. Close and latch them before leaving — weather on the bay can change quickly.
9. **Trash:** Bags are under the sink. Building trash chute is in the hallway (door marked TRASH). Do not leave bags in the hallway.
10. **Shoes:** Please remove shoes at the entry mat. The original timber floors are beautiful and scratch easily.

## Kayak Use

Two sit-on-top kayaks are stored in the lower boathouse. Access with the **red fob** on the hook by the front door.

- Paddles, life jackets, and dry bags are stored alongside the kayaks.
- Please rinse kayaks with the hose after saltwater or heavy use. (The hose is coiled on the dock post.)
- Return kayaks to the boathouse and lock the door when done.
- Kayaks are not to be taken off Lakeford Bay without notifying the host.
- **Always wear a life jacket on the water.** It's Minnesota law for children; it's just smart for adults.

## Gas Fireplace (Wall Unit)

The wall-mounted gas fireplace is located in the living area, below the mounted TV.

1. Find the **black remote** on the mantel shelf.
2. Press **POWER ON** — the igniter will click 2–3 times and the flame will appear.
3. Use **FLAME HEIGHT ▲/▼** to adjust intensity.
4. Press **POWER OFF** to turn off.
5. The fireplace has an automatic shutoff after 4 hours. To reset, simply press POWER ON again.

> **Note:** The fireplace is decorative with supplemental heat — not the primary heat source. For main heating, use the Nest thermostat on the kitchen island.

## Building Notes & Quirks

- **The building is 80 years old.** The floors creak — that's charm, not a structural problem.
- **Water pressure:** Excellent, but the building uses a shared hot water system. Very early mornings (5–7 AM) can have a brief lag before hot water arrives. Give it 60 seconds.
- **The elevator:** There is a small freight elevator off the main lobby — great if you have heavy bags. It's slow but reliable. Press and hold the floor button until it moves.
- **Fog and humidity:** Lakeford Bay is misty in the mornings. It's gorgeous. Close windows at night to prevent condensation on the floors.
- **Seagulls:** They're locals. Don't leave food on the deck or they will notice immediately and never forget.
