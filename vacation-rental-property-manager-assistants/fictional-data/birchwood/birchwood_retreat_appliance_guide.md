## ☕ Coffee Maker — SolBrewTM Pour-Over Brewer

**Location:** Kitchen counter, next to the window
**Model:** SolBrew Artisan Series Pour-Over Brewer (SB-POA6)

This is a **single-serve pour-over style brewer** that produces a richer, cleaner cup than a standard drip machine. It works with ground coffee or a reusable filter — both are stocked in the cabin.

### How to Brew (Standard Method)

1. Place the ceramic dripper on top of your mug (the dripper lives on the shelf above the coffee station).
2. Insert a **#2 cone filter** from the brown box in the cabinet above — OR use the stainless steel reusable filter in the small tin to the right.
3. Add **2 tablespoons of ground coffee** for a standard 10–12 oz mug. Coarser grind = smoother flavor; finer = more intense.
4. Fill the kettle with fresh cold water and place on the stovetop burner. Heat to just before a rolling boil (ideal: 195–205°F). Do not use boiling water — it makes the coffee bitter.
5. Pour a small amount of hot water (just enough to wet the grounds) and wait 30 seconds. This is called the **bloom** and releases gases for a better flavor.
6. Continue pouring slowly in a circular motion until your mug is full.
7. Total brew time: 3–4 minutes.

### Electric Kettle Option
There is also an electric gooseneck kettle on the counter. Fill it, switch it on, and it will hold temperature at 200°F using the **HOLD** button. Perfect for pour-over.

### Coffee Supplies
- **Ground coffee:** In the sealed canister on the counter (house blend from Kettle & Cup Roasters in town — yes, it's the good stuff).
- **Whole beans + hand grinder:** In the cabinet above, for those who want to grind fresh.
- **Filters:** #2 cone filters in the brown box; reusable stainless filter in the tin.

### Cleaning
- Rinse the ceramic dripper and reusable filter with hot water after each use.
- Paper filters are compostable — compost bin is under the sink.
- Descale the electric kettle monthly with a vinegar-water solution if mineral deposits appear.

## 🍟 Air Fryer — NordicAir Compact 4 QT

**Location:** Left side of the kitchen counter
**Model:** NordicAir Compact Fryer (NA-4CX)

### Overview
This compact 4-quart unit is ideal for 1–4 people. It heats up in under 3 minutes and produces excellent results on everything from frozen foods to fresh vegetables and proteins.

### Basic Operation

1. Remove the basket by pulling the handle straight out.
2. Place food in the basket. **Do not stack — single layer for best results.** Lightly spray or brush with oil for best browning.
3. Slide the basket back into the unit until it clicks firmly.
4. Press **POWER** (top left button — it glows blue when on).
5. Use the **+ / –** buttons to set temperature. Range: 170°F to 400°F.
6. Use the **TIME + / TIME –** buttons to set cook time (up to 60 minutes).
7. Press **START** — the fan begins immediately.
8. Shake the basket halfway through cooking for most foods (press **PAUSE**, pull out basket, shake, reinsert, press **START** again).
9. Three beeps signal the cycle is done. The unit powers off automatically.

### Quick Reference Cooking Guide

| Food | Temp | Time | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Frozen french fries | 380°F | 14–16 min | Shake at 7 min |
| Chicken thighs | 375°F | 22–25 min | Internal temp: 165°F |
| Salmon fillet | 390°F | 9–11 min | Skin side down |
| Frozen burritos | 350°F | 15 min | Flip halfway |
| Asparagus | 375°F | 8–10 min | Toss with olive oil |
| Leftover pizza | 325°F | 4–5 min | Restores crispy crust! |
| Bacon | 350°F | 10–12 min | Foil-line basket for easy cleanup |
| Frozen waffles | 340°F | 5–6 min | Better than the toaster |

### Cleaning

1. Unplug and cool completely (minimum 20 minutes).
2. Wash basket and tray with warm, soapy water or top rack of dishwasher.
3. Wipe interior with a damp cloth — never submerge the main unit.
4. Wipe exterior with a dry cloth.
5. Do NOT use abrasive scrubbers on the non-stick basket.

### Troubleshooting

| Problem | Solution |
|---|---|
| Won't power on | Check basket is fully inserted; check plug |
| Not getting crispy | Reduce quantity; ensure food is patted dry; increase temp slightly |
| White smoke | Fatty drippings burning on heating element; clean the unit |
| Error light flashing | Unplug for 60 seconds, then restart |
| Food uneven | Shake basket more frequently; avoid piling food |

## 🫧 Washer & Dryer — ClearSpring Stacked Laundry Center

**Location:** Utility closet off the hallway (sliding door)
**Model:** ClearSpring Stacked Laundry Center (CS-27ST)

This is a **stacked, all-in-one laundry center** — the washer is on the bottom, dryer on top. They share one plug and one control panel.

> ⚠️ **HE Machine — Use only HE (High-Efficiency) detergent.** HE pods and liquid detergent are on the shelf above the unit. Regular detergent causes overflow suds and error codes.

---

### WASHER (Bottom Unit)

#### Loading & Starting

1. Open the **porthole door** on the left side of the washer (pull the handle firmly — it has a tight seal).
2. Load clothes loosely — do not pack tightly. Max capacity: ~12 lbs (front-load units are efficient with smaller loads).
3. Pull out the **detergent drawer** at the top of the washer panel:
   - **I (left):** Pre-wash detergent (optional)
   - **II (center):** Main detergent — use 1 HE pod or fill liquid to the line
   - **⚘ (right, flower symbol):** Fabric softener
4. Push drawer in firmly.
5. Turn the **program selector** dial:
   - **Cotton 60°** – Standard everyday loads (hot water)
   - **Cotton 40°** – Regular loads (warm water, energy efficient — recommended)
   - **Synthetics 40°** – Blends, polyester, activewear
   - **Delicates 30°** – Gentle cycle; cold water
   - **Wool/Handwash** – Very gentle; cold water
   - **Rinse + Spin** – Rinse only, no detergent
   - **Spin Only** – Extract water, no rinse
6. Press **START/PAUSE** — the door locks and the cycle begins.
7. Cycle time is shown on the digital display. A full cotton cycle is approximately 55–75 minutes.
8. When done, the display reads **END** and the door unlocks after a 2-minute pause. Do not force the door open.

#### Temperature Note
Lower temperatures (30°C/40°C) are gentler on fabrics and significantly more energy-efficient. We recommend the **Cotton 40°** setting for most loads.

---

### DRYER (Top Unit)

#### Important: Clean Lint Filter Before Every Load
The lint filter slides out from the **top left front of the dryer drum opening**. Pull it up and peel off the lint. Discard in the trash. This is a fire safety requirement.

#### Operating the Dryer

1. Transfer wet laundry from washer to the dryer drum above. Do not overfill.
2. Slide the **lint filter** back in firmly.
3. Turn the **dryer program selector** dial:
   - **Cotton High** – Towels, sheets, heavy items
   - **Cotton Low** – Everyday clothes
   - **Synthetics** – Blended fabrics, activewear (medium heat)
   - **Delicates** – Low heat for gentle items
   - **Refresh/Air Dry** – No heat; tumbles to remove wrinkles and freshen
   - **Timed Dry** – Choose 20, 40, or 60 minutes manually
4. Press **START**.
5. Unit beeps when cycle is complete.
6. Remove clothes promptly to prevent wrinkles. If wrinkled, run the **Refresh** cycle for 10 minutes.

#### Pro Tips
- **Wool dryer balls** are on the shelf above (the orange ones) — toss 2–3 in to reduce static and speed drying.
- A **dry towel added to a wet load** can reduce drying time by 15–20 minutes.
- Do not dry anything marked **Dry Flat** or **Line Dry Only** — lay these on the drying rack in the closet.

### Troubleshooting — Both Units

| Problem | Unit | Solution |
|---|---|---|
| **E4** error | Washer | Check water supply hoses behind unit are fully open |
| **E9** error | Washer | Unbalanced load; redistribute clothes and restart |
| **F6** error | Dryer | Clean lint filter; check that vent is not obstructed |
| Clothes still wet | Dryer | Ensure lint filter is clean; run additional timed cycle |
| Door won't open (washer) | Washer | Wait 3 minutes after cycle ends for auto-unlock |
| Excessive vibration | Washer | Redistribute load; ensure machine is level |
| Unit not starting | Both | Check that power cord is fully plugged in |
