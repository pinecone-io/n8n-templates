## ☕ Coffee Maker — BrewMaster Pro 12

**Location:** Kitchen counter, left of the sink
**Model:** BrewMaster Pro 12 (BM-12X)

### Getting Started

1. Fill the water reservoir on the left side of the machine. The MAX line is clearly marked — do not overfill.
2. Open the filter basket (swing arm on the front). Insert a #4 cone paper filter (stored in the cabinet above) OR use the reusable gold mesh filter already in the drawer.
3. Add coffee grounds: use **1 tablespoon per 6 oz of water** as a baseline. We stock ground coffee in the canister on the counter — help yourself!
4. Close the filter basket until it clicks.
5. Press the **BREW** button (large button, center of panel). The indicator light will turn orange while brewing and green when complete.
6. The carafe has a **Keep Warm** function for up to 2 hours automatically. To turn it off early, press and hold **BREW** for 3 seconds.

### Brew Strength Settings
- **MILD** – Press the cup icon once (light glow)
- **REGULAR** – Default setting (no extra press needed)
- **BOLD** – Press the cup icon twice (bright glow)

### Schedule a Brew
1. Press **PROG** button
2. Use **▲/▼** arrows to set desired brew time
3. Make sure water and grounds are loaded the night before
4. Press **PROG** again to confirm — the clock icon will appear

### Cleaning
- **Daily:** Rinse the carafe and filter basket with warm water.
- **Weekly:** Run a brew cycle with just water to flush the system.
- **Monthly:** Fill the reservoir with equal parts white vinegar and water, run a full brew, then run two cycles with clean water. Vinegar is in the pantry cabinet.

### Troubleshooting
| Problem | Solution |
|---|---|
| Machine won't turn on | Check the power cord is fully plugged into the wall |
| Coffee tastes weak | Use Bold setting or add more grounds |
| Water not flowing | Descale with vinegar (see cleaning above) |
| Carafe leaks | Ensure the carafe is fully seated on the warming plate |

## 🍟 Air Fryer — CrispKing 6.5 QT

**Location:** Kitchen counter, right side near the window
**Model:** CrispKing Deluxe 6.5 QT (CK-65D)

### Overview
This air fryer fits a full pound of french fries, a small whole chicken, or multiple servings of just about anything. It circulates superheated air for crispy results with little to no oil.

### Basic Operation

1. Pull the basket straight out from the unit.
2. Place food in the basket in a **single layer** for best results. Do not overcrowd.
3. Slide the basket back in until it clicks.
4. **Turn the left dial** to set temperature (180°F – 400°F).
5. **Turn the right dial** to set time (1–60 minutes).
6. The unit begins heating immediately. The fan runs throughout cooking.
7. **Shake the basket halfway through** cooking for most items — pull the basket out, give it a shake, and slide back in.
8. When the timer reaches zero, it beeps 5 times and shuts off automatically.

### Quick Reference Cooking Times

| Food | Temp | Time | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Frozen french fries | 380°F | 15–18 min | Shake at 8 min |
| Chicken wings | 380°F | 20–25 min | Shake at 12 min |
| Salmon fillet | 400°F | 10–12 min | Don't overcook |
| Frozen pizza rolls | 370°F | 8–10 min | Shake halfway |
| Broccoli florets | 375°F | 12 min | Toss with a little oil |
| Bacon strips | 350°F | 10–12 min | Line basket with foil |
| Reheating leftovers | 325°F | 4–6 min | Restores crispness! |
| Frozen mozzarella sticks | 360°F | 6–8 min | Shake halfway |

### Tips
- A light spray of cooking oil improves browning on many items.
- Parchment paper liners (in the kitchen drawer) prevent sticking and make cleanup faster.
- The basket and tray are **dishwasher safe** (top rack only).
- Do not use aerosol sprays (like PAM) directly on the basket — they damage the non-stick coating over time. Use a brush or misting bottle instead.

### Cleaning
1. Unplug and allow to cool completely (at least 30 minutes).
2. Remove basket and tray. Wash in warm soapy water or dishwasher.
3. Wipe interior with a damp cloth. Never submerge the main unit.
4. Wipe exterior with a dry cloth.

### Troubleshooting
| Problem | Solution |
|---|---|
| Unit won't start | Basket must be fully inserted and clicked into place |
| Food not getting crispy | Don't overcrowd; pat food dry before cooking |
| White smoke | Food is too fatty; place a slice of bread under basket to absorb grease |
| Blue smoke | Residue burning; clean the unit thoroughly |
| Beeping after cook | Normal — 5 beeps indicate cycle is complete |

## 🫧 Washer & Dryer — TideMaster HE Pair

**Location:** Mudroom, off the kitchen
**Models:** TideMaster HE Washer (TM-450W) + TideMaster HE Dryer (TM-450D)

> ⚠️ **Note:** These are high-efficiency (HE) machines. Always use **HE laundry detergent** (labeled HE). Regular detergent causes excessive suds and may trigger an error code. HE pods and liquid detergent are under the sink.

---

### Washer — TideMaster HE Washer (TM-450W)

#### Basic Operation
1. Load clothes into the drum. Do not pack tightly — leave room for clothes to tumble. Max load = 16 lbs (roughly a full laundry basket).
2. Add **HE detergent** to the dispenser drawer (pull it out from the top left):
   - **Left compartment:** Detergent
   - **Middle compartment:** Fabric softener (optional — Downy is provided)
   - **Right compartment:** Pre-soak / bleach (only if needed)
3. Close the door firmly until it clicks.
4. Turn the **Cycle Selector** dial to choose a cycle:
   - **Normal** – Everyday cottons and mixed loads
   - **Delicates** – Light fabrics, lingerie
   - **Heavy Duty** – Towels, jeans, dirty work clothes
   - **Quick Wash** – Lightly soiled items, ~30 minutes
   - **Rinse & Spin** – No detergent; just rinse and spin out water
5. Press **START/PAUSE** to begin.
6. The machine will lock the door during the cycle. Do not force it open.
7. When done, the machine beeps and the DONE light illuminates. Remove laundry promptly to prevent mildew.

#### Adjusting Settings
- **Water Temp:** Press **TEMP** button to cycle through Cold / Warm / Hot. We recommend Cold for most loads — saves energy and is gentler on clothes.
- **Spin Speed:** Press **SPIN** button. High = less drying time. Low = better for delicates.
- **Extra Rinse:** Press **EXTRA RINSE** for sensitive skin or heavily soaped loads.

#### Troubleshooting
| Code/Issue | Meaning | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| **E1** | Door not fully closed | Push door firmly until click |
| **E3** | Excessive suds | Run a rinse & spin cycle; use HE detergent only |
| **E5** | Unbalanced load | Open, redistribute clothes, restart spin |
| **F2** | Water supply issue | Check that both water valves behind machine are fully open |
| Vibrating loudly | Unbalanced load | Redistribute items evenly |
| Musty smell | Mildew buildup | Run **Tub Clean** cycle (under Extra settings) monthly |

---

### Dryer — TideMaster HE Dryer (TM-450D)

#### Basic Operation
1. **Clean the lint trap before every load.** The lint trap pulls out from the front door rim. Peel the lint off and discard in the trash. Failure to clean the lint trap is a fire hazard and reduces efficiency dramatically.
2. Transfer wet clothes from washer. Don't overfill — clothes need room to tumble.
3. Turn the **Cycle Selector** dial:
   - **Regular/Heavy** – Cottons, towels, jeans
   - **Permanent Press** – Synthetics, blends (ends with a cool-down to prevent wrinkles)
   - **Delicate/Air Dry** – Low heat for gentle items
   - **Timed Dry** – Manual time control (turn the Time dial)
   - **Air Fluff** – No heat; just tumbles to fluff items
4. Adjust **HEAT** level if desired (High / Medium / Low / No Heat).
5. Press **START**.
6. Machine beeps when done. Remove clothes promptly to minimize wrinkles.

#### Pro Tips
- Throw a **dry towel** in with a wet load to speed up drying time significantly.
- Wool dryer balls are in the cabinet above — they reduce static and dry time. 3–4 balls per load.
- Don't over-dry — it causes shrinkage and wears out fabrics.

#### Troubleshooting
| Problem | Solution |
|---|---|
| Clothes still damp | Clean lint trap; check that vent hose isn't kinked |
| Takes very long to dry | Load too large; clean lint trap; split into two loads |
| Won't start | Door not fully closed; press firmly |
| Burning smell | Stop immediately; check for debris in drum; clean lint trap |
| Static electricity | Use wool dryer balls or a dryer sheet |
